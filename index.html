Delicacies

Tempura

Erase whatever you have in mind, because Dumaguete’s Tempura is not the deep-fried shrimp dish of the Japanese. Tempura is very similar to Kikiam. Major difference is its length and taste.

Kwek-Kwek

Dumaguete has a lot of street foods around the area. Mostly near the schools and universities. Some vendors usually transfer from one place to another because they use their bicycles for transportation.

BudBod

Bodbod is Negors Oriental’s version of Suman. Paired with a cup of hot chocolate, Budbod becomes the star in every Negrense’s breakfast table.




Halo Halo

This can be found inside the Dumaguete Public Market. They have a very unique taste of halo-halo. Bading’s halo-halo is known for serving halo-halo with sweetness and creamy goodness.

Balut

Is a popularly known Filipino delicacy made from incubated duck eggs.

Beef Balalo

Soup or stew of beef shanks with bone marrow inside the bone with cabbage, pechay and corn.
